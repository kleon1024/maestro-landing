[Singapore Budget 2026](/blog/singapore-budget-2026-ai-guide-eis-psg-champions) expanded the Enterprise Innovation Scheme (EIS) to include AI expenditures, giving businesses a 400% tax deduction on qualifying AI spending for Years of Assessment 2027 and 2028. For SMEs making their first serious AI investments, this represents one of the most generous tax incentives available anywhere in the world for AI adoption.

But the headline number -- 400% -- can be misleading without understanding the mechanics. This article breaks down exactly what the deduction means, how it translates to real dollar savings, what qualifies, and how to document your AI spending to withstand IRAS scrutiny.

## The Math: What 400% Actually Means

A 400% tax deduction does not mean the government pays you four times what you spend. It means you can deduct four times the qualifying amount from your taxable income. The actual cash benefit depends on your corporate tax rate.

  <Metric value="SGD 50,000" label="Maximum qualifying AI expenditure per YA" />
  <Metric value="SGD 200,000" label="Maximum tax deduction per YA (50K x 400%)" />
  <Metric value="SGD 34,000" label="Maximum tax saving per YA (at 17% corporate rate)" />
  <Metric value="68%" label="Effective recovery rate on qualifying AI spend" />
</MetricGrid>

### Worked Example 1: Maximum Benefit

A Singapore SME spends SGD 50,000 on qualifying AI expenditures in its financial year ending 31 December 2026 (YA 2027).

| Line Item | Amount |
|-----------|--------|
| Qualifying AI expenditure | SGD 50,000 |
| Enhanced deduction (400%) | SGD 200,000 |
| Additional deduction above normal (300% x 50K) | SGD 150,000 |
| Tax saving on additional deduction (17% x 150K) | SGD 25,500 |
| Normal deduction tax saving (17% x 50K) | SGD 8,500 |
| **Total tax saving** | **SGD 34,000** |

The company spends SGD 50,000 and recovers SGD 34,000 through reduced tax liability. The effective net cost of the AI investment is SGD 16,000.

### Worked Example 2: Below the Cap

An SME spends SGD 30,000 on qualifying AI expenditures.

| Line Item | Amount |
|-----------|--------|
| Qualifying AI expenditure | SGD 30,000 |
| Enhanced deduction (400%) | SGD 120,000 |
| Additional deduction above normal (300% x 30K) | SGD 90,000 |
| Tax saving on additional deduction (17% x 90K) | SGD 15,300 |
| Normal deduction tax saving (17% x 30K) | SGD 5,100 |
| **Total tax saving** | **SGD 20,400** |

The effective net cost is SGD 9,600 for SGD 30,000 of AI capability.

### Worked Example 3: Above the Cap

An SME spends SGD 80,000 on qualifying AI expenditures.

| Line Item | Amount |
|-----------|--------|
| Qualifying AI expenditure | SGD 80,000 |
| Enhanced deduction on first 50K (400%) | SGD 200,000 |
| Normal deduction on remaining 30K (100%) | SGD 30,000 |
| Total deduction | SGD 230,000 |
| Tax saving on enhanced portion (17% x 150K additional) | SGD 25,500 |
| Tax saving on normal deductions (17% x 80K) | SGD 13,600 |
| **Total tax saving** | **SGD 39,100** |

<Callout type="info" title="Key Insight">
Spending above the SGD 50,000 cap still receives the normal 100% tax deduction. The 400% enhanced rate applies only to the first SGD 50,000 of qualifying AI expenditure per YA. If you have significant AI spending planned, consider timing your investments across YA 2027 and YA 2028 to maximize the enhanced deduction over both years -- potentially recovering up to SGD 68,000 across the two-year window.
</Callout>

## What Qualifies as AI Expenditure

The expansion of EIS to cover AI expenditures is new under Budget 2026. Understanding what IRAS considers qualifying AI expenditure is critical for a successful claim.

### Likely Qualifying Expenditures

Based on the EIS framework and Budget 2026 announcements, the following categories of AI spending are expected to qualify:

**AI Software Development and Customization**
- Development of custom AI models or algorithms for business use
- Fine-tuning or adapting pre-trained AI models to specific business needs
- Integration of AI capabilities into existing business systems
- Development of AI-powered applications and tools

**AI Platform and Tool Licensing**
- Subscription fees for AI development platforms and tools
- Licensing costs for AI/ML frameworks and libraries
- Cloud computing costs directly attributable to AI model training and deployment
- API costs for AI services integrated into business operations

**AI Consulting and Integration Services**
- Fees paid to AI consultants for strategy development and implementation
- Technical integration services connecting AI tools to existing workflows
- AI solution architecture and design services
- Custom AI solution development by external vendors

**AI-Related Training (When Tied to Development)**
- Technical training for staff directly involved in AI development or deployment
- Certification programmes for AI/ML engineering skills
- Specialized workshops on AI implementation methodologies

### What Likely Does Not Qualify

<Callout type="warning" title="Common Pitfalls">
Not all technology spending counts as AI expenditure. IRAS distinguishes between genuine AI activities and general IT spending. Mislabeling non-AI costs as AI expenditures risks audit complications and potential penalties.
</Callout>

- General IT infrastructure upgrades (servers, networking) unless specifically for AI workloads
- Standard software licensing (CRM, ERP, office tools) without AI components
- Data storage costs not directly related to AI model training
- General staff training not specifically focused on AI development or deployment
- Marketing automation tools that use rule-based logic rather than machine learning
- Hardware purchases for general business use

### The Grey Area

Some expenditures fall in a grey area where qualification depends on the specific use case:

- **Data engineering**: Qualifies if the data pipeline is specifically built to feed AI models. Does not qualify if it serves general business reporting.
- **Cloud computing**: Qualifies for compute hours used for AI model training and inference. General cloud hosting does not qualify.
- **Hybrid solutions**: If a solution combines AI and non-AI components, only the AI-attributable portion qualifies. You must be able to separate and document the AI-specific costs.

## Documentation Requirements

Proper documentation is the difference between a smooth claim and an audit headache. IRAS expects clear evidence that expenditures relate to genuine AI activities.

### What to Document

<StepGuide>
  <Step number={1} title="AI Project Description">For each qualifying expenditure, maintain a written description of the AI project it supports. Include the business problem being solved, the AI technique or approach used, and the expected outcome.</Step>
  <Step number={2} title="Expenditure Breakdown">Keep itemized invoices and receipts that clearly identify the AI-related nature of the work. Generic descriptions like 'IT consulting' or 'software development' are insufficient. Ensure vendors reference AI specifically in their invoices.</Step>
  <Step number={3} title="Technical Evidence">Maintain evidence that AI technology was genuinely involved. This could include model training logs, API usage records, AI platform subscription confirmations, or technical architecture documents showing AI components.</Step>
  <Step number={4} title="Allocation Methodology">If expenditures are shared between AI and non-AI activities (e.g., cloud computing used for both), document the methodology used to allocate costs. Use a reasonable and consistent basis such as compute hours or project time tracking.</Step>
  <Step number={5} title="Timeline Records">Record when expenditures were incurred to confirm they fall within the qualifying YA period. For multi-year projects, document the portion of work completed within each financial year.</Step>
</StepGuide>

### Vendor Invoice Best Practices

When engaging AI solution vendors, request that invoices explicitly reference:

- The AI nature of the work performed (e.g., "AI model development" not "software development")
- Specific AI technologies or approaches used
- The business application or use case
- Clear separation of AI and non-AI components if applicable

This documentation serves as primary evidence for IRAS and significantly simplifies the claiming process.

## Timing Strategy: Maximizing the Two-Year Window

The EIS AI expansion covers YA 2027 and YA 2028. For most companies with a standard January-to-December financial year, this means qualifying expenditure incurred in calendar years 2026 and 2027.

### Option 1: Concentrate Spending

If your AI needs are below SGD 50,000 total, spend it all in one financial year to simplify documentation and claiming.

### Option 2: Split Across Both Years

If your planned AI investment exceeds SGD 50,000, consider splitting it across both qualifying years to maximize the enhanced deduction:

| Strategy | Year 1 (YA 2027) | Year 2 (YA 2028) | Total Enhanced Deduction | Total Tax Saving |
|----------|-------------------|-------------------|--------------------------|------------------|
| All in Year 1 | SGD 100,000 | SGD 0 | SGD 200,000 | SGD 34,000 |
| Split evenly | SGD 50,000 | SGD 50,000 | SGD 400,000 | SGD 68,000 |
| **Difference** | | | **SGD 200,000** | **SGD 34,000** |

<Callout type="info" title="Double the Benefit">
By splitting SGD 100,000 of AI spending across two years instead of concentrating it in one year, a business can double its enhanced tax deduction from SGD 200,000 to SGD 400,000 -- an additional SGD 34,000 in tax savings. For any AI investment exceeding SGD 50,000, a phased approach is financially optimal.
</Callout>

### Option 3: Accelerate Planned Spending

If your business has AI investments planned for 2028 or beyond, consider accelerating them into the YA 2027-2028 window to capture the enhanced deduction before it expires. The time value of the tax saving typically outweighs the cost of earlier-than-planned implementation.

## How to Claim: Step by Step

<StepGuide>
  <Step number={1} title="Track Qualifying Expenditure Throughout the Year">Do not wait until tax filing time to identify qualifying AI spending. Maintain a running register of AI-related expenditures with supporting documentation from the start of the financial year.</Step>
  <Step number={2} title="Verify Expenditure Against Qualifying Criteria">Before claiming, review each expenditure against the EIS qualifying criteria. Remove any items that fall into grey areas unless you have strong supporting documentation.</Step>
  <Step number={3} title="Calculate the Enhanced Deduction">Apply the 400% rate to qualifying expenditure up to the SGD 50,000 cap. Calculate the normal deduction for any excess. Determine total deduction and resulting tax saving.</Step>
  <Step number={4} title="Include in Corporate Tax Return (Form C / Form C-S)">Report the enhanced deduction in your corporate tax return. Include the EIS claim in the relevant section of the form. Attach the expenditure schedule and supporting summary.</Step>
  <Step number={5} title="Retain Documentation for Five Years">IRAS may request supporting documentation at any time within the statutory period. Retain all invoices, project descriptions, technical evidence, and allocation methodologies for at least five years from the YA of the claim.</Step>
</StepGuide>

## Practical AI Investments for SMEs Under SGD 50,000

For SMEs looking to maximize the EIS benefit, here are realistic AI investments that typically fall within the SGD 50,000 annual cap:

**AI-Powered Customer Service (SGD 10,000-25,000)**
Deploy AI chatbots or virtual assistants that handle routine customer inquiries, reducing response times and freeing staff for complex issues.

**Document Processing and Extraction (SGD 15,000-35,000)**
Implement AI-based document processing for invoices, contracts, or compliance documents. Particularly relevant for firms in accountancy and legal sectors.

**Predictive Analytics (SGD 20,000-45,000)**
Build AI models that forecast demand, customer churn, or inventory needs. Custom models tailored to your business data deliver the highest ROI.

**AI-Assisted Content and Communication (SGD 5,000-15,000)**
Integrate AI writing and editing tools into marketing, communications, or documentation workflows.

**Custom AI Solution Development (SGD 30,000-50,000)**
Engage an AI solutions partner to build a tailored AI application that addresses a specific business challenge. This category offers the strongest qualifying position under EIS since the AI nature of the work is self-evident.
